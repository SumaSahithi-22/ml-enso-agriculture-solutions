La Niña Historical Data
La Niña events are characterized by cooler sea surface temperatures in the central Pacific Ocean. Historical data helps in understanding their effects on global weather.
Year Intensity Global Impact
2010-2011 Strong Heavy rains in Australia, drought in the southeastern United States
1998-2001 Moderate Increased hurricane activity in the Atlantic, wetter conditions in East Africa
1988-1989 Weak Cooler temperatures in the Pacific, agricultural impacts in various regions
1984-1985 Moderate Dry conditions in South America, droughts in Southeast Asia
1973-1974 Strong Dry conditions in Australia, floods in parts of South America
1967-1968 Weak Cold winter in North America, wet conditions in the southwestern United States
1954-1956 Moderate Increased rainfall in the eastern Pacific, agricultural impacts
1949-1950 Strong Effects on Southeast Asia and Australia, flooding in South America
