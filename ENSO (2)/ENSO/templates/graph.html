Below is the graphical representation of ENSO trends over time.

Description:

    The image shows a graph titled "ENSO and ONI Relation". The graph represents the relationship between the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) and the Oceanic Niño Index (ONI). The ONI is a measure of sea surface temperature anomalies in the central and eastern tropical Pacific Ocean.
  • El Niño is characterized by warmer-than-average sea surface temperatures in the central and eastern Pacific Ocean.
  • La Niña is characterized by cooler-than-average sea surface temperatures.
  • The graph shows that there have been both El Niño and La Niña events over the past several decades.
  • The ONI has fluctuated significantly over time, being above 0.5 for El Niño conditions and below -0.5 for La Niña conditions.
