Oceanic Niño Index (ONI) Prediction and Forecast

Description:

The graph illustrates the Oceanic Niño Index (ONI) trends over time, spanning from 2017 to 2025, with actual, predicted, and forecasted values. The ONI is a key measure used to monitor El Niño and La Niña events, which impact global climate patterns.

Legend:

  • Black Line: Actual recorded ONI values.
  • Blue Line: Predicted values based on our LSTM model, closely following the actual trend but with slight deviations.
  • Red Line: Forecasted ONI for late 2024 to 2025, showing a downward trend.

Observations:

  • The ONI fluctuates over time, showing periodic rises and falls, indicative of El Niño (positive values) and La Niña (negative values) phases.
  • Around 2019-2020, a notable dip is observed, aligning with a La Niña phase, followed by a sharp decline in 2021.
  • A strong El Niño event is visible in 2023-2024, where the ONI peaks above 1.5.
  • The forecast suggests a declining ONI into 2025, indicating a transition away from El Niño conditions.
